# Executive Summary

Between 2020 and 2023, the global workforce experienced significant transformation driven by disruptions such as COVID-19, rising inflation, and rapid technological shifts. During this period, many companies—especially in the tech sector—entered a phase of aggressive growth, expanding their workforce to meet surging demand and capitalize on new market opportunities.

However, this rapid expansion soon proved unsustainable. As economic conditions tightened in 2022, organizations across the globe began implementing large-scale layoffs, affecting thousands of employees. The impact was not uniform, with certain industries and regions experiencing significantly higher job losses than others, highlighting deeper structural and economic challenges.

Given this sudden shift from rapid hiring to widespread layoffs, it becomes crucial to understand what really happened. Which industries were most affected? How did layoffs evolve over time? And what key factors ultimately triggered these large-scale workforce reductions?
